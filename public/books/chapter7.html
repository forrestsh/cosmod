7.1 Honest Boundaries

A serious theoretical framework must not only state clearly what it can say; it must honestly mark what it cannot. The Cosmic Grid currently exists at the conceptual framework stage; many of its core claims have yet to receive adequate mathematical formalization or empirical support. This chapter identifies six of the most important open problems and briefly sketches the current state of each, along with possible directions for future progress.

7.2 Open Problem 1: How Does the FCC Lattice Give Rise to Continuous Spacetime?

Cosmic Grid holds that continuous spacetime is a large-scale emergent approximation of the FCC lattice, but the precise mechanism of this emergence has yet to be mathematized. The core challenge: the FCC lattice has fixed directional anisotropy (propagation is preferred along the 12 nearest-neighbor directions) — how does isotropic Lorentz-invariant spacetime emerge from it exactly in the macroscopic limit? Work in Loop Quantum Gravity and Causal Dynamical Triangulations (CDT) provides reference paths: at sufficiently large scales, the statistical average of discrete structures can yield a smooth Riemannian geometry. Cosmic Grid needs to develop the mathematical tools to show, concretely, that the FCC lattice in the thermodynamic limit yields (3+1)-dimensional Minkowski spacetime.

7.3 Open Problem 2: How Does Quantum Behavior Emerge from Binary Signals?

The core features of quantum mechanics — superposition, entanglement, wavefunction collapse — have yet to receive a clear explanation within the Cosmic Grid framework. Binary signals are classical (0 or 1); how do quantum probability amplitudes emerge from them? One possible direction: quantum superposition corresponds to multiple possible trajectories in the evolution of lattice rules, rather than a single determinate one; wavefunction collapse corresponds to the rule selection that occurs when a consciousness node interacts with physical lattice nodes. This line of thought has potential resonances with certain quantum consciousness theories (such as the Penrose-Hameroff Orchestrated Objective Reduction theory), but requires extensive mathematical work to make precise.

7.4 Open Problem 3: Is the Creator's Excitation Signal Physically Detectable?

Cosmic Grid holds that the Creator continuously excites the lattice network through external vibration signals transmitted at a meta-level outside the lattice. This raises a fundamental question: is this excitation signal in principle detectable by observers within the universe? If the signal is completely undetectable, it is physically equivalent to nonexistence, and the framework's falsifiability is called into question. If it is detectable, we must describe its observable effects concretely — for instance, whether lattice evolution under certain specific conditions exhibits patterns that cannot be explained by purely internal rules. This is the key problem for moving the framework toward physical testability.

7.5 Open Problem 4: The Physical Interface Mechanism of Consciousness Nodes

This book holds that consciousness nodes interact with the FCC lattice network through the physical body (the brain and nervous system), but the precise mechanism of this interface has yet to be described. How does consciousness read lattice signals? How do consciousness's decisions write into the lattice network, thereby influencing physical evolution? One possible direction: the consciousness node couples to the lattice network through a special class of lattice state patterns (the neural correlates of consciousness); the consciousness's free choices influence lattice evolution by modulating these patterns. The precise description of this mechanism will require interdisciplinary collaboration among neuroscience, physics, and the philosophy of mind.

7.6 Open Problem 5: The Origin and Distribution of Consciousness Nodes

This book describes the structure and function of consciousness nodes, but has not answered: How do consciousness nodes come into being? Do all sufficiently complex material systems generate consciousness nodes, or are consciousness nodes separately created by the Creator? Do animals, artificial intelligence systems possess consciousness nodes? These questions directly concern the boundaries of consciousness — among the most intensely debated areas in contemporary philosophy of mind. The Cosmic Grid framework provides a conceptual language (consciousness node, rule uniqueness, inhabitation relation), but cannot yet provide unambiguous criteria. The question of consciousness node origins may ultimately require a theological claim: consciousness is a specific endowment of the Creator, not a spontaneous product of material complexity.

7.7 Open Problem 6: The Path to Mathematical Formalization

The path from conceptual framework to a computable, testable physical theory requires several key steps of mathematical development:

Field theory on the FCC lattice: construct a rigorous lattice field theory on the FCC lattice, studying the dispersion relation of signal propagation and its continuum limit.

Algebraic structure of the rule space: systematically study the group-theoretic structure of 12-input/12-output binary rules, especially the algebraic properties of the bijective rule group.

Proof of emergent geometry: rigorously prove that the FCC lattice in the thermodynamic limit yields (3+1)-dimensional Lorentz-invariant geometry.

Mathematical model of the consciousness interface: develop a mathematical framework describing the coupling of consciousness nodes to physical lattice nodes.

Connection to the Standard Model: systematically compare the particle-emergence picture of Cosmic Grid with the known structure of the particle physics Standard Model.

7.8 Chapter Summary

Six core open problems span the full boundary of the Cosmic Grid framework — from physics (emergent spacetime, quantum behavior, detectability) to consciousness (interface mechanism, node origins) to mathematics (the path to formalization). The existence of these problems is not a weakness of the framework but an honest marking of the boundaries of what it can currently do, while pointing the way toward future research. A framework with clear boundaries is epistemically more valuable than one that claims to encompass everything.
